# Research Plan: Realist Approaches in Health Policy and Systems Research

## Research Questions
1. **What is realism as a methodological and philosophical paradigm?**
   - Historical roots (critical realism, scientific realism)
   - Core principles and concepts (ontology, mechanisms, context, causation)
   - Key theorists and foundational works

2. **What are the foundational concepts specific to realist approaches?**
   - Mechanisms and context (CMO: Context-Mechanism-Outcome)
   - Causal reasoning in realist frameworks
   - Realism vs. other methodologies (positivism, constructivism, pragmatism)

3. **How is realism applied to health policy research?**
   - Realist evaluation methods
   - Policy implementation and intervention research
   - Key examples and case studies

4. **How is realism applied to health systems research?**
   - Systems thinking under realism
   - Organizational and institutional factors
   - Key examples and frameworks

5. **What are practical implications for health researchers?**
   - Research design guidance
   - Data collection and analysis methods
   - Common challenges and how realists address them

## Strategy

**Evidence types needed:**
- Foundational academic papers on critical realism and realist methodology
- Health policy and systems research papers explicitly using realist approaches
- Papers discussing realist evaluation methods
- Comparison/contrast papers (realism vs. other paradigms)
- Books/chapters on realist methodology

**Search angles:**
1. Academic papers: realism, critical realism, realist evaluation, realist methodology
2. Health policy applications: realist approaches to policy analysis, implementation science
3. Health systems applications: realist approaches to health systems, organizational theory
4. Practical guidance: research design, methods, case studies
5. Foundational theorists: Roy Bhaskar, Margaret Archer, Ray Pawson, etc.

**Time period:** Foundational works (1970s–1990s) + contemporary applications (2000–present)

**Parallel research angles (4 researchers):**
- **R1**: Foundational realist philosophy and methodology (critical realism, realist evaluation)
- **R2**: Realism in health policy research (policy analysis, implementation, evaluation)
- **R3**: Realism in health systems research (systems thinking, organizational approaches)
- **R4**: Practical methods and comparative positioning (research design, comparison with other approaches)

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Core concepts of realism clearly defined with primary source backing
- [ ] At least 3 foundational works identified and summarized
- [ ] At least 5 health policy examples found and characterized
- [ ] At least 5 health systems examples found and characterized
- [ ] Distinction from other approaches (positivism, constructivism) explained with sources
- [ ] No critical claim rests on only one source
- [ ] Contradictions (if any) between sources identified and addressed
